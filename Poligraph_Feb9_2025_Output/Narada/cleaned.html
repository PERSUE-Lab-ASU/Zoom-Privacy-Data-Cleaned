Your California Privacy Rights

If you are a resident of California, you have specific rights regarding your personal information under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA). These rights include:

  • Right to Know: You have the right to request that we disclose certain information to you about our collection and use of your personal information over the past 12 months. This includes the categories of personal information collected, the categories of sources from which the personal information is collected, business or commercial purpose for collecting or selling that information, the categories of third parties with whom that personal information is shared with, and the specific pieces of personal information collected about you.
  • Right to Delete: You have the right to request that we delete any of your personal information that we collected from you and retained, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to Correct: You have the right to request that we correct any inaccurate personal information that we maintain about you.
  • Right to Opt-Out: You have the right to opt out of the sale or sharing of your personal information. We do not sell personal information, but if we engage in any activity that might be construed as selling or sharing personal information, we will provide an opt-out mechanism.
  • Right to Limit Use and Disclosure of Sensitive Personal Information: You have the right to request that we limit our use and disclosure of your sensitive personal information to the specific purposes allowed under the CPRA.
  • Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your CCPA or CPRA rights. Unless permitted by the CCPA or CPRA, we will not deny you services, charge you different prices, or provide a different level of service based on the exercise of your rights.

You can make a request to access your personal information or exercise other rights mentioned above by submitting a request to us by emailing privacy@narada.ai. We will deliver our response to your request either electronically or through postal mail. Please note that we are only obligated to respond to your requests for information twice within a 12-month period.

We do not sell personal information pertaining to California residents. If, in the future, our activities might be construed as selling or sharing personal information under CCPA, we will provide an opt-out mechanism through a “Do Not Sell My Personal Information” link on our website, ensuring that California residents can exercise their right to opt-out. Furthermore, personal information is only shared for business purposes, as detailed in the “Third-Party Service Providers” section of this Privacy Policy.
