What Data We Collect
  • Data You Give Us

    We do not collect any user personal data. User enroll the device using the customer provided PIN and no user personal details are collected or processed.

  • Data We Collect

    The information we collect is necessary for our business function and activities. To provide TechPulse products, we must process information about the device. This application is primarily intended for corporate owned Android devices. By signing up for our service and accepting our terms and conditions customers have indicated that they want us to collect analytics on their devices, including Location Information.

    We automatically collect the below set of device information periodically.

    • Location information. We collect device location information periodically even when the application is in background. Location is collected only when the customer enables the location tracking and end user provides the consent through OS permissions dialog.
    • Battery Information. We collect data about device battery information. This data includes battery health, capacity, and temperature.
    • Memory Information. We collect device memory information. This data includes device total memory and free memory.
    • Storage Information. We collect device details about storage units on the device. We capture storage capacity, available free space, and type of storage unit such as internal vs external.
    • Device/System information. We collect device-specific information. This includes information such as device operating system, device serial number, device name, processor details, processor architecture, OS version and CPU vendor.
    • Network Usage Statistics. We collect details about how network is used over a period. This data includes network carrier details, amount of data received and transmitted over a period.
    • App Usage Statistics. We collect application usage data from the device. This data includes amount of time a particular application is in foreground over a period and amount of storage space consumed by the app.
    • Installed Applications. We collect data about installed applications on the device. This includes system apps and user apps.
How We Use Data

We use the information we collect for following purposes, including:

  • Location information. HP TechPulse is a device analytics platform that customers purchase to track and view key metrics about their fleet of devices by location. Access to the user’s location in the Android OS is essential to our customers’ ability to group and manage the devices by their location in HP TechPulse. Primarily, location is used to protect the lost device.
  • Battery. To provide information on battery replacement recommendation based on battery health of devices enrolled in Proactive Management.
  • Device Utilization. To provide information about devices that have high storage and memory utilization, and the software applications causing high CPU and memory utilization.
  • Storage Capacity. To forecast ahead the time duration left prior to the disk space reaching a predetermined threshold
  • Hardware Health. To show a monthly trend of suboptimal devices as unhealthy and facilitates drilling down to identify devices failing to meet individual performance metrics (i.e. battery health, disk health, thermal health, network, applications, memory, and storage space utilization).
  • Mobility Factor. To provide information on the mobility aspects of devices by classifying devices into various mobility factor categories and grouping device mobility factor by hardware health. Unhealthy devices with highest mobility factor may require attention by IT as opposed to unhealthy devices with low mobility factor.
  • Non-Reporting Devices. To provide the hardware inventory of devices by last seen status in Proactive Management.
  • Software Inventory. To enable the IT administrator to view the operating system version and applications installed on devices, including the following:
    • When an application was last updated.
    • The top applications installed within the fleet.
    • Applications updated/installed within this week, last week, or last month.
  • Software Utilization. Provides information on the top twenty-five software applications that are most used across all devices.
