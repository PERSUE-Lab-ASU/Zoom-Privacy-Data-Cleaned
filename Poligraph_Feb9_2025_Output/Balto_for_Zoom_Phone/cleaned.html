Updated: October 3, 2024

Balto Software, Inc. (“Balto”) provides cloud-based, AI-powered software that provides real-time guidance to contact centers.  In this Privacy Notice we refer to the Balto hosted software and the suite of services provided through the hosted software as the “Balto Solution.” We maintain websites to market the Balto Solution at www.balto.ai and other websites controlled by us (collectively, the “Website”).

The Balto Solution is a business-to-business solution offered in the United States. We license the Balto Solution to entities that operate call centers (our “Clients”).  Clients can access our Solution through our website at https://baltocloud.com. “Authorized Users” are persons who work for a Client and are authorized to access the Balto Solution on behalf of the Client.  

Balto knows you care about how your personal information is used and disclosed and your privacy is a top priority for us.  We gather information about persons who interact with our Website and the Balto Solution, people who attend our marketing events, and job candidates to enhance our business. Our Privacy Notice helps you understand what information we collect and receive and how we use it and the choices you have. This Privacy Notice does not cover the practices of companies we don’t own or control. 

Please read this Privacy Notice carefully to understand our policies and practices regarding your information and how we will treat it. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, your choice is not to use our Website, purchase our goods or services, provide us with your personal information, or otherwise interact with us. By accessing or using this Website, providing us with your personal information, purchasing our goods or services, or otherwise interacting with us, you agree to this Privacy Notice. 

As described herein, this Privacy Notice may change from time to time. Your continued use of the Website, our products or services, provision of your personal information, or other interactions with us after we make changes is deemed to be acceptance of those changes, so please check the Privacy Notice periodically for updates.

In this Privacy Notice personal information or “PI” means information that identifies, relates to or describes a particular person and information that is capable of being associated with, or could reasonably be linked, directly or indirectly, with a particular person. 

There are four sections to this Privacy Notice:

  1. PI we collect and why we collect it.
  2. How we disclose PI and why we disclose it.
  3. How we use online tracking technologies including cookies.
  4. General concepts that apply to the PI we collect or receive.

1. PI we collect and why we collect it.

The Balto Solution. The Balto Solution will provide Balto with information related to call center calls of Balto’s Clients. The specific types of information, including PI, provided are determined by the way the related Client has configured and uses the Balto Solution and are outside of Balto’s control. PI may be contained in the content of communications Client or its Authorized Users generate using the Balto Solution and Balto does not and cannot control the content of such communications when generated. When Balto processes any such content, Balto is as a processor or service provider acting on behalf of Client. Further, Balto limits its use of personal information to the minimum required to provide and improve the Balto Solution. For example, if you are an Authorized User then you have the option of enabling call center call audio recording or transcription, or both. When you do so, Balto captures and stores call center audio recordings and transcriptions in order to provide the Balto Solution, By default, the Balto Solution is structured to exclude any content from call center audio recordings and transcriptions that is personal information through a rigorous redaction and scrubbing process that begins as soon as the information is generated. However, subject to due diligence, certain Clients may be allowed to change this default setting so that the Balto Solution will store an unredacted version of call center audio recordings and transcriptions for review by the Client’s Authorized Users. Depending on the PI involved, Balto may require the Client to store such unredacted call center audio recordings and transcriptions outside of the Balto Solution.

Within the last 12 months, the personal information we have collected or received includes the following categories of personal information set forth under the California Consumer Protection Act, as amended from time to time (the “CCPA”): identifiers, California Customer Records personal information, commercial information, Internet or other similar network activity, geolocation data (not precise geolocation data), sensory data such as audio and electronic information, inferences drawn from other personal information, and, for job candidates, professional or employment-related information and potentially non-public education information. Within the last 12 months, we have disclosed these categories of personal information for the business purposes listed above and in accordance with Section 2 below.

3. How we use online tracking

When you visit our Website, Balto and our third-party service providers acting on our behalf automatically collect certain data using tracking technologies like cookies, web beacons, and similar technologies.

Like many website owners, Balto and our marketing partners, affiliates, or analytics or service providers, use automated data collection tools such as cookies and similar technologies to collect certain information, in order to, among other things, analyze trends, administer the Website, tracking users’ movements around the Website, and to gather demographic information about our user base as a whole.

“Cookies” are small text files that are placed on your hard drive by a website’s computers. We may use session Cookies, persistent Cookies, and Web Beacons (defined below). We use Cookies to identify that you’ve accessed the Website. We also use these tracking technologies to help us better understand your behavior and facilitate and measure the effectiveness of our Website.

We also use small electronic files known as web beacons (also referred to as clear gifs, pixel tags, and single-pixel gifs) (“Web Beacons”)to collect information about your engagement with us. Web Beacons are tiny graphics that can recognize certain types of information on your computer, such as Cookies, when you view a particular website tied to the Web Beacon, and a description of a website tied to the Web Beacon. Web Beacons are embedded on web pages or in an e-mail.

All modern Internet browsers allow you to control your cookie settings. You can set your browser to refuse all or some browser cookies, or to alert you when cookies are being sent. Please note that certain features of the Website will not be available or will not function properly once cookies are disabled. For further information about cookies and how to manage them, please visit www.allaboutcookies.org.

Here is a description of the types of cookies Balto uses:

Required cookies. Required cookies, including session cookies, make it possible for you to access the Website, navigate within the Website, and access information related to your account. Required cookies are necessary to operate the Website, so you can’t opt-out of them.

Functionality Cookies. Functionality cookies allow the Website to remember information you have entered or preferences you select, and provide enhanced, more personal features. You can use your browser settings to opt-out of functionality cookies. Note that opting out may affect the functionality of our Website for you.

Performance Cookies. These cookies collect data about how visitors use the Website. This includes data like which pages visitors go to the most. These cookies don’t collect information that individually identifies visitors. The data these cookies collect is aggregated and intended to be de-identified and used to improve how the site functions and performs. Balto uses Google Analytics to help analyze user behavior on the Website. Information on opting out of tracking technologies from Google Analytics is here https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout?.
