By using Passageways Websites, or voluntarily providing your information to us, you consent to the collection and use of all information as set forth in this Privacy Policy. If you (or the Associated Entity) have an account with Passageways for the Services, this Privacy Policy is incorporated into and subject to the Master Subscription Agreement governing your use of such Services.

APPLICABILITY OF THIS PRIVACY POLICY

This Privacy Policy applies solely to information and data you may provide to us or we otherwise collect from you (or the Associated Entity) via Passageways Websites, whether you are a casual visitor to Passageways Websites or have an account with Passageways for use of the Services (whether directly or through an Associated Entity). This Privacy Policy does not apply to (i) information that may be collected by Passageways through any other means, including offline or (ii) information about you collected by third party services, applications, or website or advertisements associated with, linked to or otherwise accessible from Passageways Websites. The information collected or received by such third parties, is subject to their own privacy policies or practices and under no circumstances is Passageways responsible or liable for the third party’s compliance therewith.

HOW DO WE COLLECT INFORMATION FROM YOU?

We collect personally identifiable information we receive through your use of Passageways Websites in several ways: (i) when you register for Passageways’ Services through Passageways Websites, (ii) when you (or the Associated Entity) use the Services, and (iii) when you elect to contact us electronically to request a trial, ask a question or for other reasons.

We also receive automatic and passive data (both personally identifiable and otherwise) from the computer used to access Passageways Websites and when you interact with Passageways Websites. In this regard, Passageways Websites use common automated information gathering tools such as logs files that record page activity, “one-pixel gifs,” “clear gifs,” or “web beacons” which are small bits of code embedded in Passageways Websites to gather certain information, and/or “cookies.” When you visit Passageways Websites, whether or not you are a registered user of the Services (as “Registered User”), Passageways’ servers send a “cookie” to your computer. Cookies are small strings of text that are sent by Passageways Websites to your browser or device and then stored by your browser on your computer’s hard drive or device. Passageways uses cookies that are session-based (“Session Cookies”) and persistent-based (“Persistent Cookies”). Session Cookies exist only during one session. They disappear from your computer when you close your browser software or turn off your computer. Persistent Cookies remain on your computer after you close your browser or turn off your computer. To the extent Passageways Websites contains any third party-provided features or functions, such features or functions may also send cookies from such companies or their advertising partners to your browser (known as “third party cookies”). Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but it is possible to change your browser setup so that it warns you each time a cookie is being sent, blocks third party cookies or blocks or rejects all cookies. However, rejecting cookies may prevent you from taking advantage of certain portions of Passageways Websites.

WHAT INFORMATION DOES THE COMPANY COLLECT FROM YOU?

Via Passageways Websites, Passageways collects from you: (i) certain non-personal technical and other information and (ii) certain personally identifiable information.

All Visitors to Passageways Websites – In general, if you visit Passageways Websites without being a Registered User for any of the Services, you will be using Passageways Websites anonymously; no personally identifiable information is collected. Whether or not you are a Registered User, when you visit Passageways Websites various types of information are collected automatically and passively through cookies, log files and other tools as noted above under the caption “How Do We Collect Information From You,” including, but not limited to the following:

  • the domain name and browser you use to access the Internet
  • the date and time of your visit
  • the pages you visited, and how many “hits” a particular item is receiving and links “clicked”
  • the address of the web site you visited immediately prior to visiting Passageways Websites
  • your IP address (a number assigned to your computer when you use the Internet)
  • your operating system
  • name of your internet service provider
  • broad demographic data
  • data relating to malfunctions or problems occurring when you use your computer with Passageways Websites and other customer service information
  • information about your use of the Services, and particular features thereof, and how frequently you use them

Standing alone, cookies do not personally identify you; they merely recognize your computer. Unless you choose to identify yourself to Passageways, either by responding to a promotional offer, opening an account, becoming a Registered User or filling out a Web form (such as a “Contact Me” or a “30 Day Free Trial” Web form), you remain anonymous to Passageways.

Discretionary Contact With Us: Passageways Websites allow you to contact us electronically through e-mail, to request a demo or other information. Whenever you make such contact, you will be providing us with some or all of the following information: your name, email address, phone number and any information you choose to include in the text of your message. If you send an e-mail message to Passageways, initiate a chat session, or complete a questionnaire, form, or other online survey found on Passageways Websites, Passageways will maintain the information in its databases.

Registered Users –If you are a Registered User of the Services, you (or someone on your behalf) will be providing us with some or all of the following registration-related information: full name, address, phone numbers, e-mail address, website address, and financial qualification and billing information, such as billing name and address, payment information, and the number of employees and others within or associated with the Associated Entity.

In addition, Registered Users or authorized persons within the Associated Entity will provide to us data, documents, materials, and information to be posted, distributed, or received by Users of Passageways Websites Site(s) (collectively, “Posted Content”).

In addition, Registered Users also may choose to submit additional information to the Services, such as uploading photos or other images, or providing information about themselves in their profiles, including optional diversity-related data (e.g., how they identify with respect to gender, race/ethnicity, sexual orientation, veteran and disability status or similar characteristics, if they wish to self-identify). When you or other Registered Users use the Services, certain data associated with your use of the Services will be collected such as feature usage, product settings and configuration, activity records, and associated log data and metadata, including, but not limited to:

  • the number of meetings
  • the number of agenda sections
  • the number of files uploaded to the agenda
  • the number of groups
  • the number of active users
  • the number of pending/inactive invites
  • the number of approvals
  • the number of attachments per approval
  • the number of resources
  • the number of announcements
  • the number of actions in a meeting

Tracking: We do not engage in the collection of personally identifiable information about your online activities over time and across third party website or online services, and we do not allow third parties to collect personally identifiable information from Passageways Websites as part of their tracking of such online activities. Accordingly, we do not currently process or comply with any web browser’s “do not track” signal or other mechanisms that provide consumers with the ability to exercise choice regarding the collection of personally identifiable information about your online activities over time and across third party website.

HOW IS YOUR INFORMATION USED?

Our Use of Information. We collect, retain, and use the information we collect via Passageways Websites for legitimate business purposes only. This not only allows us to provide the Services as they exist today, but it also allows us to provide you with innovative features and services we develop in the future. For example, we may use the information we receive about you:

to provide you with our Services;

  • to help keep Passageways Websites safe and secure, and protect users’ rights or property;
  • to monitor your use of Passageways Websites, including to enforce our Terms of Use; and
  • to correspond with you regarding our Services or matters relating to your use or potential use of Passageways Websites, or to inform you of developments in our company or with our Services that we believe may be of interest to you. For example, if you fill out a “Contact Me” Web form, Passageways will use the information provided to contact you about your interest in the Services. Passageways may also use information we collect for products and event information dissemination purposes.

We use cookies and other automated and passive tools (such as log files) to gather broad demographic information for internal marketing and demographic studies; to help identify you as a Registered User and to track and personalize your use of Passageways Websites so as to improve your user experience; to assess overall activity on Passageways Websites, including tracking interest in particular features of Passageways Websites and our Services; and for internal operations, including troubleshooting, diagnosing problems with servers, data analysis, testing, research and service improvement. We use such information for statistical/analytical purposes and for evaluating our Services to make them more useful to customers and to track and aggregate non-personal information. For example, Passageways uses IP addresses to monitor the regions from which Registered Users and visitors navigate Passageways Websites. Passageways also collects IP addresses from Registered Users when they log into the Services as part of Passageways’ “Identity Confirmation” and “IP Range Restrictions” security features.

Sharing of Information. Your trust is important to us, which is why we don’t share information we receive about you with others. Moreover, we do not sell or rent your personally identifiable information to third parties for marketing purposes.

However, notwithstanding the foregoing, we may share your personal information in limited situations as follows:

  • to protect and defend our rights, property, or safety or those of our Registered Users, or others; and in connection with the enforcement of terms which may govern Passageways Websites or provision of the Services;
  • with our affiliates or business firms contracted to provide specific services to us or on our behalf, in a manner consistent with this Privacy Policy. In situations such as this, the affiliate or third party servicing firm will be contractually obligated to use your personal data only for the purpose for which our relationship with the firm exists, and the firm will not have the right to share your data with other organizations or contact you outside the bounds of their contract with us. However, we cannot guarantee compliance by any such third party firm;
  • when we believe it is appropriate to investigate, prevent, or take action regarding illegal or suspected illegal activities (such as for potential fraud or infringement);
  • when required to do so by law or regulation, or in response to legal process (for example, a court order, search warrant or subpoena) or a request from a law enforcement or governmental agency or authority, and in other circumstances in which we have a good faith belief that a crime has been or is being committed by you or another User or that an emergency exists that poses a threat to the safety of you or another person; and
  • aggregate statistical information with respect to “traffic” patterns and usage of Passageways Websites may be provided to our partners or affiliates; provided that such information has been rendered non-personally identifiable.

Strictly Necessary Cookies

Always Active

These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you which amount to a request for services, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in or filling in forms. You can set your browser to block or alert you about these cookies, but some parts of the site will not then work. These cookies do not store any personally identifiable information.

Performance & Analytics Cookies

Active

These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. They help us to know which pages are the most and least popular and see how visitors move around the site. All information these cookies collect is aggregated and therefore anonymous. If you do not allow these cookies we will not know when you have visited our site, and will not be able to monitor its performance.

Functional Cookies

Active

These cookies enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalisation. They may be set by us or by third party providers whose services we have added to our pages. If you do not allow these cookies then some or all of these services may not function properly.

Marketing Cookies

Active

They do not store directly personal information, but are based on uniquely identifying your browser and internet device. If you do not allow these cookies, you will experience less targeted advertising.
