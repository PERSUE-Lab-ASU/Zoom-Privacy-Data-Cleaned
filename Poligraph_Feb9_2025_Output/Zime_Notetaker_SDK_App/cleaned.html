8. Data Breach Reporting Process
In the event of a data breach, we are committed to maintaining transparency and open communication with our users. Our data breach reporting process is designed to protect our users and comply with applicable laws and regulations.
  • Identification and Assessment: Upon discovering a suspected data breach, we will promptly investigate to determine the scope and impact of the incident. We will work to identify the cause of the breach and take immediate steps to secure our systems and prevent further unauthorized access.
  • Notification: If we determine that your personal data has been compromised, we will notify you without undue delay, in accordance with applicable legal requirements. We will provide you with information about the nature of the breach, the categories of data involved, the likely consequences, and the measures we are taking to address the breach.
  • Reporting to Authorities: We will report the data breach to the appropriate supervisory authority within the timeframe required by law, typically within 72 hours of becoming aware of it, unless the breach is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals.
  • Mitigation and Prevention: We will take steps to mitigate the effects of the breach and prevent future occurrences. This may include enhancing our security measures, updating our policies, and providing additional training to our staff.
  • Continuous Improvement: We will conduct a thorough review of the incident and our response to it, in order to improve our data protection and breach response processes.

11. CONTROLS FOR DO-NOT-TRACK FEATURES
Most web browsers and some mobile operating systems and mobile applications include a Do-Not-Track ("DNT") feature or setting you can activate to signal your privacy preference not to have data about your online browsing activities monitored and collected. At this stage no uniform technology standard for recognizing and implementing DNT signals has been finalized. As such, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals or any other mechanism that automatically communicates your choice not to be tracked online. If a standard for online tracking is adopted that we must follow in the future, we will inform you about that practice in a revised version of this privacy notice.

CCPA Privacy Notice
The California Code of Regulations defines a "resident" as:
(1) every individual who is in the State of California for other than a temporary or transitory purpose and
(2) every individual who is domiciled in the State of California who is outside the State of California for a temporary or transitory purpose
All other individuals are defined as "non-residents."
