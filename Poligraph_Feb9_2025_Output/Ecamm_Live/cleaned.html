Facebook API

The Ecamm Live application makes use of Facebook API Services. The application stores an access token on your Mac, which allows it to upload video content to the Meta platform.

The Ecamm Live Service may access or process, but not store, the following Facebook and Instagram Platform Data for the purposes of live stream production:

  • Your avatar image.
  • The name of Facebook Pages that you administer.
  • The name of Facebook Groups that you belong to.
  • The name and avatar image of Instagram Business Pages that you administer.
  • The title, post description, permalink, and date of scheduled live streams.
  • Viewer reactions and number of concurrent viewers associated with your current live stream.
  • Viewer comments associated with your current live stream, including each commenter’s name and avatar image.

The Ecamm Live Service may access, process, or store the following Facebook and Instagram Platform Data for the purposes of live stream production:

  • Your name.
  • The date, time, and Facebook Group name associated with your most recent live streams.
  • Diagnostic data related to accessing of Platform Data.

Stored Facebook and Instagram Platform Data is deleted:

  • Whenever Platform Data is no longer necessary to provide an app experience or service to users.
  • After 30 days.
  • When requested by a user by contacting Ecamm Network.
  • When a user deletes their account associated with the Ecamm Live.
  • When requested by Meta.
  • When required by law or regulation.

Users can revoke the application's access to Facebook and Instagram Platform Data via the Facebook’s Business Integration Settings.

Users can request modification or deletion of stored Facebook and Instagram Platform Data by contacting Ecamm Network.

Users can contact Ecamm Network with questions or complaints about these privacy practices.

Use of the Facebook features are subject to the Facebook Terms of Service.

Use of the Instagram features are subject to the Instagram Terms of Use.
