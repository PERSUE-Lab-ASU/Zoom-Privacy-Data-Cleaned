  1. How We Track You

7.1. Cookies

We use “cookies” to help you personalize your online experience. A cookie is a text file that is placed on your hard drive by a Web server.

You have the ability to accept or decline cookies. Most Web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can usually modify your browser setting to decline cookies if you prefer. If you choose to decline cookies, some features and functionality of our website may not operate as expected. If you view our website without changing your cookie settings, you are indicating your consent to receive all cookies from the website.

7.2. Log Files

We may collect information related to your activity on our websites, such as your IP address, browser type, domain names, access times and referring website addresses. This information is used by us for monitoring the operations of our services, to maintain the quality of our services, and to provide general statistics regarding use of our websites.

7.3. Clear Gifs and Related Technologies

Our website and emails also use pixel tags, web beacons, clear GIFs, or other similar technologies to measure the success of marketing campaigns, to help us manage the operation of our services and to compile statistics about usage and response rates, and other purposes. Clear gifs are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies, and are used to track the online movements of internet users. In contrast to cookies, which are stored on your hard drive, clear gifs are embedded invisibly on Web pages or in emails and are about the size of the period at the end of this sentence.

7.4. Analytics Services

We utilize third party analytic services (e.g., Google Analytics) to help us continually improve our services and track your interactions with our websites. For Google Analytics, you may opt out by visiting the opt-out webpage for Google Analytics (https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout) and installing the applicable add-on for your web browser.

7.5. Do Not Track and the Network Advertising Initiative

“Do Not Track” refers to a preference you may set in your browser that sends a signal to websites indicating your desire not to be tracked. We do not currently support “Do Not Track” or other similar browser preferences. However, you can visit the Network Advertising Initiative opt-out page located at: https://optout.networkadvertising.org if you would like to set up opt-out preferences that limit or remove tailored online ads provided by third parties who track your online activity.
