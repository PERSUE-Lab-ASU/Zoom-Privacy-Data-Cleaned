Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) Compliance

‍

Purpose and Scope
In compliance with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), this section outlines our practices regarding the privacy of student educational records.
FERPA governs the confidentiality and access to student records maintained by educational institutions.

‍

Educational Records
We recognize student educational records as defined by FERPA and ensure the protection of personally identifiable information contained within these records. Examples of educational records may include marks and attendance records.

‍

Access Controls
Access to student educational records is restricted to authorized personnel only. We utilize role-based access controls to limit access to individuals with legitimate educational interests, ensuring the confidentiality of student data.

‍

Educator and Business Owner Responsibility for Consent
Educators and business owners who utilize our platform to conduct online classes or operations are responsible for obtaining written consent from eligible students or their parents before disclosing any educational records, except as permitted by FERPA. By utilizing our platform, educators and businesses acknowledge and accept responsibility for complying with this requirement and ensuring that necessary consent is obtained as per regulatory requirements.

‍

Data Security
We implement stringent security measures to safeguard student educational records from unauthorized access, disclosure, or alteration. Our security protocols are regularly reviewed and updated to maintain the confidentiality and integrity of student data.

‍

Parent and Student Rights
Parents and eligible students have the right to review and request amendments to educational records. We provide a transparent process for submitting requests and ensure timely responses to inquiries regarding student data.

Cookies

When You use the Platform, We try to make that experience simple and meaningful. In order to do so, We use Cookies which are small pieces of information that are issued to Your computer or mobile device when You visit a website or access or use a mobile application, and that stores and sometimes tracks information about Your use of the Platform. These files contain a small amount of data, which may include an anonymous unique identifier. In general, cookies are sent to Your browser from a web site and stored on Your computer's hard drive. We use them to recognize Your browser and to provide a more personalized experience for You which includes personalized advertisements on other websites and storage of items in Your Cart between visits thereby ensuring that Your experience with Us is simplified. When You visit Our Platform and/or use Our Services, Our web server sends a cookie to Your computer or mobile device.

Please note that some of the cookies used by the Platform are set by us, and some are set by third parties who are delivering services on Our behalf.

We use cookies for other reasons as well. Like many sites, We too use cookies to collect information. We also use Cookies to allow you to enter your password less frequently during a session. You can instruct Your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. However, if You do not accept cookies, You may not be able use some portions of the Platform and/or Services. If You do leave cookies turned on, be sure to sign off when You finish using a shared computer.
