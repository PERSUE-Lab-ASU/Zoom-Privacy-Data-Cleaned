(a) Completely support and enhance your visit to our Website.

(b) Allow you to access your account information without requiring you to answer your sign-in question multiple times during the same visit to our Website.

(c) Ensure that you won’t see the same promotions too often.

(d) Better measure how many users view and navigate our Website.

(e) Better measure how effective our online marketing campaigns are performing.

If you wish, you may set your browser to refuse cookies. However, by refusing to accept a cookie, you will not be able to access some pages on CallCabinet. Since many websites use some form of cookie technology, setting your browser to refuse cookies may restrict your access to certain information or product offerings on those websites. Some CallCabinet cookie files remain on your computer’s hard drive until you manually delete the file. CallCabinet cookie files do not contain personally identifiable information or track an individual users’ movement through our Website. A CallCabinet third-party advertiser cookie file may also be placed on your hard drive during your visit to our Website. The purpose of these types of cookies is to collect aggregate statistics about your visit to our Website. These cookies do not contain personally identifiable information or track an individual users’ movement through our Website.

10. GDPR Disclosure

You may exercise these rights to the extent these rights apply to you by contacting CallCabinet via email. We will respond to your request within the time frame prescribed by applicable law.  Although CallCabinet will make reasonable efforts to accommodate your requests, in some circumstances we may deem your request unfounded or not eligible under applicable law. If that should happen, CallCabinet expressly reserves the right to refuse your personal data request. Before CallCabinet is able to accommodate any request, provide you with any information or correct any inaccuracies we will verify your identity by requesting certain information or identification.

In the instance of CallCabinet acting as a “data processor” or “service provider”, you will need to exercise your rights directly with the “data controller”.

GDPR Provides Individuals with Eight Rights:

(a) The right to be informed

Organizations need to tell individuals what data is being collected, how it’s being used, how long it will be kept and whether it will be shared with any third parties.  This information must be communicated concisely and in plain language.

(b) The right to access

Individuals can submit data access requests, via email which obliges CallCabinet to provide a copy of any personal data concerning the individual. CallCabinet has one month to produce this information, although there are exceptions for requests that are manifestly unfounded, repetitive or excessive.

(c) The right to rectification of inaccurate personal data

If the individual discovers that the information CallCabinet holds on them is inaccurate or incomplete, they can request that it be updated. As with the right to access, CallCabinet has one month to do this, and the same exceptions apply.

(d) The right to erasure (also known as ‘the right to be forgotten’)

Under certain circumstances, individuals can request that CallCabinet erase their data such as when the data is no longer necessary, the data was unlawfully processed or it no longer meets the lawful ground for which it was collected. This includes instances where the individual withdraws consent.

(e) The right to restrict processing of your personal data

Individuals can request that organizations limit the way they use personal data. It’s an alternative to requesting the erasure of data, and might be used when the individual contests the accuracy of their personal data or when the individual no longer needs the information but the organization requires it to establish, exercise or defend a legal claim.

(f) The right to data portability

Individuals are permitted to obtain and reuse their personal data for their own purposes across different services. This right only applies to personal data that an individual has provided to the “data controller” by way of a contract or consent.

(g) The right to object to the processing of personal data for direct marketing purposes

Organizations must stop processing information unless they can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds for the processing that overrides the interests, rights and freedoms of the individual or if the processing is for the establishment or exercise of defense of legal claims.

(h) Rights related to automated decision making including profiling

The GDPR includes provisions for decisions made with no human involvement, such as profiling, which uses personal data to make calculated assumptions about individuals.

There are strict rules about this kind of processing, and individuals are permitted to challenge and request a review of the processing if they believe the rules aren’t being followed.
