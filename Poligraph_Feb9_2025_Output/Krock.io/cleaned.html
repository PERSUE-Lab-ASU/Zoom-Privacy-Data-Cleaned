How We use cookies

We use “cookies” and similar technologies. Cookies is a text-only string of information that a website transfers to the cookie file of the browser on a user’s computer hard disk. This occurs so that the website can remember who the user is. Cookies will typically contain the name of the domain from which the cookies have come, the ‘lifetime’ of the cookies, and a value, usually a randomly generated unique number. Cookies may also be stored in your internet browser by or on behalf of third parties, such as Google.

When you visit the Service, the Service sends you cookies. Cookies enable us to record anonymous information about you, including: the type of internet browser you use to access the Service, your computer operating system, the websites you visited before visiting the Service, the areas of the Service you visit and your IP address (which can provide information about your approximate geographic location). Cookies cannot be used to disclose your identity.

We may use cookies in order to learn how the Service is being used and which types of individuals are using the Platform; record and report user behaviour, location, audience and interest data; record which other websites have referred You to the Service; optimize the Service and improve it; make it easier for you to fill in forms on the Service.

We will retain information received from Our use of cookies indefinitely.

If You do not wish for Us to use cookies when You access the Service, please adjust Your Internet browser settings to decline cookies (your computer’s help file should tell you how to do this). If You choose to decline cookies, some parts of the Service may not work properly. By using the Service, You agree to Our use of cookies as described above.

We do not use Google advertising cookies, therefore We do not share them with Google.
