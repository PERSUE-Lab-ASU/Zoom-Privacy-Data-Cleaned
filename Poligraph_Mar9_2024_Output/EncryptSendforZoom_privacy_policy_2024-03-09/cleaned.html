If you or your organization have an individual agreement with us, that agreement may include privacy terms that also apply to the information you provide under it. Please review those terms, as they may differ from or be more restrictive than the terms in this Privacy Policy.  

This Privacy Policy does not extend to any third-party websites and apps you may use, including those linked to within our Services. Before clicking on any links, it is essential to review the terms and policies for third-party websites and apps.  

Encrypt Send’s core products and services assist users in creating, completing, and validating digital or electronic transactions securely. We act as a secure transportation vehicle for sensitive data. As part of our Services, users request us to collect and record information that helps validate transportation transactions. This information includes the individuals involved in the transactions and the devices used by those individuals.  

Encrypt Send processes two main categories of personal information when you use our products and services:  

Your personal information as a user or customer (or potential customer) of Encrypt Send – referred to as Customer Account Data.  

The personal information of your end users who use or interact with you from Encrypt Send’s platform – this category includes both Customer Usage Data (e.g., communications metadata) and Customer Content (e.g., contents of communications).  

We process these categories of personal information differently due to the distinct relationship we have with you, our customer, versus the indirect relationship we have with your end users.

To provide and enhance our Services and support advertising and marketing efforts, we collect information about visitors to our sites, users of our Services (Customer Account Data), the devices they use, and, in some cases, their locations.  

When you visit our website or request more information about Encrypt Send, we automatically collect information using tracking technologies such as cookies and through web forms where you input your details. We use this information to fulfill your requests made through the web form.  

Certain information is collected directly from you when you fill out forms with your name or email address. Other information, like details about devices, browsers, or locations, is collected automatically without requiring your input into a form.  

You have the option to choose whether to visit our sites, install our apps, or provide information to us. However, not providing certain information may limit your ability to use some parts of our Services.  

Information You Share Directly:  

On Encrypt Send’s public-facing website, you may fill out web forms to request contact from us, subscribe to a newsletter, participate in a survey, or attend an online demo. The personal information required in these forms will vary based on the form’s purpose. We will request the necessary information to fulfill your request through the form. For example, if you want to sign up for an email newsletter, we will ask for your email address, and if you request a call from us, we will ask for your phone number. Additionally, we may request additional information to understand you better as a customer, such as your Encrypt Send use case, company name, or role at your company. If you wish to opt-out of further marketing communications from Encrypt Send, you can do so through a preferences page linked in any marketing email you receive, or you can contact our Support Team (see Sections 5 & 12 below).  

Information We Collect Automatically:  

When you visit Encrypt Send’s website, including our web forms, we and our service providers automatically collect certain information using tracking technologies like cookies, web beacons, and similar tools. This information helps us understand how visitors use our websites, which pages and features are popular, and how we can improve our websites and track the performance of our advertisements. Visitors to our website can change cookie settings at any time using their web browsers built in settings. 

What Customer Account Data We Process When You Communicate with Us 

When you communicate with us, you may share personal information, such as contact details. We retain a record of these interactions.  

Information shared during such communication, including contact details and other relevant information, is recorded by us. Additionally, this information helps manage ongoing customer relationships. While we take appropriate measures to protect any sensitive information shared with us, we advise against sharing any personal or sensitive data in these communications that are not necessary for the teams to assist you.

If you have questions or concerns about such processing, including how to exercise your data subject rights, we recommend reaching out to the relevant customer.  

Broadly, we use Customer Account Data to further our legitimate interests, which include:  

  • Understanding our customers and potential customers, along with their interests in Encrypt Send’s products and services.

HOW ENCRYPT SEND PROCESSES YOUR END USERS’ PERSONAL INFORMATION  

Your end users’ personal information may appear on Encrypt Send’s platform in several ways:  

Communications-related personal information, such as your mobile numbers, email addresses, names, or IP addresses, may be present when you use this information to contact other users through our products and services.  

Your end users’ personal information may be contained in the content of communications pushed or pulled using Encrypt Send’s products and services.  

We refer to the information in the first two points as Customer Usage Data, and the information in the third point as Customer Content.  

As mentioned earlier, privacy laws in certain jurisdictions classify data processors and data controllers. When Encrypt Send processes Customer Content, we typically act as a processor. When processing Customer Usage Data, we act as a processor in many respects but may act as a controller for certain activities, such as billing, reconciling invoices with telecommunications carriers, and troubleshooting network issues.

Encrypt Send uses Customer Usage Data and Customer Content to provide communication services and necessary functions as a communications service provider.
