Introduction

Since the entry into force of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), all persons within the European Economic Area (EEA) have been granted extensive rights regarding their personal data. Eversports recognises that the protection and careful handling of your personal data are very important. As data protection is of great importance at Eversports, Eversports complies strictly with the applicable legal provisions in Austria (GDPR, the Data Protection Act [German acronym: DSG], the Telecommunications Act [German acronym: TKG] of 2003) and in Germany (GDPR, the Federal Data Protection Act [German acronym: BDSG], the Telemedia Act [German acronym: TMG], TKG) when using these data.

Definitions

 GDPR: The EU General Data Protection Regulation. In this Privacy Statement, terms such as data subject, personal data, processing, controller, processor and recipient are all used within the meaning of Article 4 GDPR.

Eversports: Eversport GmbH with its business address at Jakov-Lind-Straße 13 H/5. OG, 1020 Vienna, Austria.

Eversports DE: Eversports Germany UG (limited liability company) with its business address at Hansaring 52, 50670 Cologne, Germany. A subsidiary of Eversports.

Appointman: APPOINTMAN UG (limited liability company) with its business address at Kaysers Kamp 3, 26188 Edewecht, Germany. A subsidiary of Eversports.

Eversports Group: Eversports and all of these subsidiaries.

Eversports Platform: The sports search engine and booking platform operated by Eversports online, in its apps and on its websites.

Sports offerings: Various services in the field of sports and leisure activities as well as sports venues that can be searched, booked and reserved via the Eversports Platform.

Users: Persons who have registered online with Eversports in order to use the sports offerings.

Sports providers: Partner companies delivering the sports offerings through their own sales channels.

Eversports service: Provision of the Eversports platform and referral of the associated sports offerings to users.

Eversports Ambassadors: Sports providers acting as consultants for the Eversports Group.

2. Data subjects

 This Privacy Statement concerns various groups of people about whom various types of data are processed. For information about the processing of your data, please read the appropriate section carefully.

    2.1. Visitors to the website

If you visit one of the Eversports websites (e.g. www.eversports.com or country-specific domains such as www.eversports.at, www.eversports.de etc. and sub-domains such as manager.eversports.com), the following information is relevant to you.

The controller (“we” or “us” in this section) for the processing of your data is Eversports.

            2.1.1. Why we process data

In connection with our online offerings, we process data from visitors to our website based on our legitimate interests in the analysis, optimisation and commercial operation of our website for the following purposes:

a)     To make the website more user-friendly for visitors;
b)     To adapt Eversports services to the preferences of visitors to the website;
c)     To verify and optimise the effectiveness of Eversports services;
d)     For purposes of troubleshooting and detection of anomalies, to guard against misuse of Eversports services and to track any unauthorised access attempts.

You may object to this processing at any time. For information about the options for objecting, please see the “Technical notes”.

            2.1.2. To whom do we transfer data?

We transfer data from visitors to the website to the following recipients and/or categories of recipient:

a)     Communication tools for support requests and communication with customers
b)     Security tools and tools for fault detection and troubleshooting
c)     External hosting providers for data storage
d)     Marketing tools
e)     Companies in the Eversports Group

d)     To enable appropriate customer support and communication;

We also process data from users for the following purposes, as part of our own legitimate interest:

f)     To verify and optimise the effectiveness of Eversports services;
g)   To display interest-based advertising on third-party service providers’ websites;
h)   To prevent misuse of Eversports services and to track unauthorised access attempts;
i)     To perform troubleshooting and roll out product improvements;
j)     To recommend products via e-mail and to provide information about new features and products from Eversport

Our legitimate interest in processing these data lies in the analysis, optimisation and commercial operation of the Eversports platform and the promotion of our products and services. You may object to this processing at any time. To do so, please contact us at the following e-mail address: [email protected]. For information about the options for objecting, please see the “Technical notes” section.

We process data from users based on consent for the following purposes:

k)     To administer newsletter recipients and dispatch newsletters;

Consent to the above purposes is given voluntarily during the registration process. The user may withdraw it at any time, with future effect, in the online profile settings. Subscription to the newsletter may also be cancelled by the user at any time, with future effect, by means of a link in the newsletter e-mails or by e-mail to [email protected].

4. Duration of the processing 

As a basic principle, your data are processed for the duration of the contractual relationship or for as long as needed for the above purposes. In addition, the data are stored only for as long as legal data retention obligations stipulate. If the use of the data is based on your consent, the data will be processed until such consent is withdrawn.

Inactive Eversports online profiles, including profile data provided by the user, are erased by default three years after the last login if they no longer have any credit.

Correspondence with prospective customers and applicants is stored for six months in case of any follow-up questions.

Server log files are stored for 30 days and then erased.

For details of the retention period for cookies and other technical tools of the Eversports online services, please refer to the “Technical notes” section.

5. Your Rights

As a basic principle, you shall have the rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability and objection. To exercise these, you can contact the controller directly. If you are not sure who is the controller for the processing of your data, you can also contact the parent group (Eversports) at any time, and your request will be directed to the right place within the group.

If a user objects to processing for purposes which are necessary for the performance of a contract, that user will no longer be able to use the Eversports services, and as a result their Eversports online profile will be erased. Services from sports providers on Eversports can still be used directly from the sports provider (e.g. local bookings), since existing credit balances with the sports providers do not expire as a result.

Subscribers to our newsletter may withdraw their consent to their subscriptions at any time, with future effect, via the link at the bottom of a newsletter or by e-mail to [email protected].

Sports providers may at any time withdraw their consent to their feedback (including their name and photograph) being displayed on the Eversports websites, with future effect, by e-mail to [email protected].

Consent to interest-based advertising on third-party service providers’ websites may be withdrawn at any time, with future effect, in the Eversports online profile settings.

In accordance with the GDPR, you shall have the right not to be subjected to a decision based solely on automated processing. The Eversports Group does not carry out automated decision-making based on the data collected.

If you believe that the processing of your data contravenes data protection law or otherwise infringes your data protection rights, you may contact the relevant controller or Eversports directly ([email protected]). We always endeavour to resolve your request to your satisfaction. You have the right to contact the supervisory authority at any time and to lodge a complaint. In Austria, this is the Data Protection Authority. A list of the competent supervisory authorities outside Austria is available here: https://www.dsb.gv.at/links
