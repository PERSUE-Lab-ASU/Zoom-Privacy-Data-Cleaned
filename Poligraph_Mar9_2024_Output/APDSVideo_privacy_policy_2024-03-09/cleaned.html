Introduction
This Privacy Policy applies to all users of the services provided by APDS, which services may be used by individuals in restrictive custody (“Learners”), the administrators (“Administrators”) and staff (“Facility Personnel”) of the facility in which the Learners are held in custody (the “Facility”), family, friends and other individuals authorized to communicate with Learners (collectively, “Family”), law enforcement personnel (“Law Enforcement”), attorneys representing Learners (“Attorneys” and collectively with Learners, Administrators, Facility Personnel, Family, and Law Enforcement, the “Authorized Users”). Services include, without limitation, APDS’ hosted software platform, mobile and desktop applications, websites and related services (collectively, the “Software”), hardware, and documentation (the “Documentation”) and other digital content, software applications, technology services, hosted websites, and secure hardware solutions for correctional institutions known as the “APDS Technology Suite” (collectively, the “Services”).

Except for specifically designated functions of the Services which explicitly state that content is designated and approved by Authorities as “Private” or “Attorney-Client Privileged” and are graphically indicated as such in a manner acceptable to APDS and the relevant Authorities, all content, messages, emails, text, audio, video, or graphics transmitted through the Services (“Content”) is subject to review by the relevant Authorities, and may be rejected as inappropriate at the sole discretion of any relevant Authority or APDS. As used herein, “Authority” shall mean the prison, jail, corrections, educational or other institution or other law enforcement or governmental authority or agency providing Users with access to the Services.

APDS does not guarantee transmission of any Content, even if you have paid APDS as consideration for transmitting Content to an individual whose internet use is restricted by the criminal justice system (a “Restricted Individual”) who has been approved to be registered as an authorized user of the Services (an “Authorized User”).

Subject to and without limiting the generality of the foregoing, at APDS we respect the privacy of those that access the Services (“Users”) and are committed to protecting it through our compliance with this Privacy Policy. To the extent your information will be held by APDS and not an applicable legal authority, we understand that we have a responsibility to protect and respect your privacy and look after your personal data. This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect.

This Privacy Policy has been developed with a view to compliance with laws regulating privacy upon those providing limited online services to Restricted Individuals incarcerated at a corrections facility, including emancipated minors and juveniles ages 13 through 17 years (“Juveniles”) and under correctional control with the consent of their applicable Authority (each an “Applicable Authority”) to use the Services and as permitted by law. APDS’ Biometric Information Privacy Policy


Last Modified: August 5, 2022

Please read this policy carefully to understand the nature of the biometric information which may be collected or transmitted through APDS’ technology, as well as the retention and destruction practices of APDS.

For purposes of this Biometric Information Privacy Policy, “Biometric Data” means any individual’s biometric identifier and/or information based on an individual’s biometric identifier used to identify that person, regardless of how it is captured, converted, stored or shared. A biometric identifier can include fingerprints, finger scans, voice recognition hand scans or face geometry/scans.

Retention & Destruction:

APDS has developed this policy to inform the Users of our Services of the nature of the potential Biometric Data that it may collect, receive, use, store, capture or otherwise obtain, as well as the purpose for which the information is obtained and the retention and destruction procedures in place for Biometric Data. APDS’ technology may collect, receive, obtain, store and use an image of an individual’s hand, finger, face or voice or data derived or extracted from a mathematical formula/algorithm from such image, specifically the use of facial recognition to log into our Learner Portal. Though it may or may not be subject to the Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act (“BIPA”), 740 ILCS 14/1, et seq., APDS nevertheless elects to provide this policy out of an abundance of caution pursuant to and in accordance with Illinois law.

Potential Biometric Data will be transmitted and stored using a reasonable standard of care for APDS’ industry and in a manner that is the same or exceeds the standards used to protect other confidential information held by APDS, including Social Security numbers, protected health information, etc. APDS will delete or destroy potential Biometric Data every ninety (90) days.

APDS will not sell, lease, trade, or otherwise profit from an individual’s Biometric Data. The potential Biometric Data that could be collected, received, used, stored, captured or otherwise obtained through the APDS system would only be shared between the User, APDS, the Applicable Authority, and any third parties with whom APDS contracts for the provision of its services. The potential Biometric Data will not be disclosed by APDS to third parties or other users unrelated to the User unless: (a) consent is obtained; (b) disclosure is required by law; or (c) disclosure is required by subpoena.

APDS reserves the right to amend this Biometric Information Security Policy at any time.
