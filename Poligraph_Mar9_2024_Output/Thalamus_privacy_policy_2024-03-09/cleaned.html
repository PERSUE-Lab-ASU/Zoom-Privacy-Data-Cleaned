This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, store, use and distribute information through any of the Thalamus services, however accessed (the “Service”), our websites and applications, including but not limited to www.thalamusgme.com (the “Site”), as well as any related documentation, software and other properties we control (together with the Service and the Site, the “Properties”). Thalamus collects personal information from you as a customer, user or visitor of our Properties.  If you are a customer or partner of Thalamus (“Customer”), Thalamus may collect personal information from your organization or your organization’s users (“End Users”) to the extent that you’ve integrated your application or services with our Properties or otherwise provided personal information to Thalamus.

In addition to this Privacy Policy, you should review our Terms and Conditions of Use, available at https://thalamusgme.com/terms/. We use defined terms in this Privacy Policy relating to the Service that are defined in the Terms and Conditions of Use.

This Privacy Policy does not apply to Thalamus employee information.

1. Consent.

By using the Service and/or visiting, browsing or using any of our Properties, you consent to the use of your information or your End Users’ information as described in this Privacy Policy.  You may choose not to provide us with the personal information listed in this Privacy Policy, but we may not be able to provide the Services to you if you choose to do so.

If you are an End User of one of our Customers and want to learn about how that Customer handles your personal information, we encourage you to read the Customer’s privacy policy. This Privacy Policy does not apply to the services that our Customers provide to their end users.

2. Collection of Information.

The Types of Information We Collect

We have several areas where you can submit information to us, and we also have features that automatically collect information from visitors and End Users of the Properties. Personal Information, as that term is used in this Privacy Policy, is any information that can be used to identify a consumer. This includes, but is not limited to: name, address, telephone number, email address, Association of American Medical Colleges (“AAMC”) ID number and/or ACGME ID, and Personal Contact Information (as defined below), and application information, such as professional references, and educational background. If we charge fees for any of the Services, we may also collect personal financial and billing information as defined below. Thalamus is not responsible for verifying the accuracy of any Personal Information that you provide or post to the Service or any of our Properties.

Information You Provide To Us

  • Registration and Service Usage Data. When expressing an interest in obtaining additional information about the Services, registering for and/or actually using the Services, we may collect personal contact information, such as your name, mailing address, phone number, AAMC or ACGME ID and email address. You will also be able to add autobiographical information to your personal Service user profile, including images, video, and other files. Since our Services include facilitating your personal interview and travel scheduling, you may also be required to input data that is personal to your interview and travel planning.  This information is referred to as your “Personal Contact Information.”
  • Commerce Functionality. We may offer premium elements of the Service and/or additional services or products. In that case, we may collect additional billing information, such as a billing address, credit card, PayPal or other billing information (“Billing Information”).
  • Sharing and Community Features. We may offer features within the Service or other Properties that allow you to further communicate with us or with other users and to participate in community features, such as forums. These features may allow you to additionally upload images, data, graphics, video, or other files.
  • Surveys, Contests. We may occasionally ask you to participate in voluntary surveys. We may also conduct contests in connection with our Properties, as part of which we may request additional information from you.

Information We Automatically Collect

Our Services may automatically collect usage and technical information about you and your usage of our Service (or other Properties). This information is used by Thalamus for the operation of the Service and Properties, to maintain quality of the Service, and to provide general statistics regarding use of the Service.  This Information may include, but is not limited to: your movement through the Service and Properties, your device type, operating system, pages served to you, the date and time of your visit or use of our Service, what elements of the Service you use and what other users you interact with, data identifying the web pages you visited prior to and after visiting our Site or use of our Service or Properties. We may also collect your IP address and other unique identifiers for your applicable mobile or other device. Collectively, this information is referred to as “Analytics Information.” Some tools that we may use to collect Analytics Information include:

  • Cookies. Cookies are small pieces of data that are stored on your computer or mobile device.
  • Pixel Tags/Web Beacons. These terms are generally used synonymously to describe small blocks of code on webpages that do things like allow another server to measure viewing of a webpage. They are often used in connection with cookies.
  • Other. We may use technology available on your device, technology similar to cookies and pixel tags and technology that is later developed, all for similar purposes.

We may also use these tools within Service-related emails that we send to you.

There are instances where we may receive information from third parties.

  • As part of their use of the Service, Administrators of Programs will pull applicant data directly from third parties that they have access to, including ERAS and ACGME, and provide it to Thalamus.
  • We may receive Personal Information from the AAMC for data analysis and research that may be conducted internally for research and development purposes, including development of improvements in existing and future products and services.
  • You may also separately provide information to websites, retailers or other commerce, product or service providers through which we provide access to register for our Service.
  • As you are using our Services, you may click through certain links or promotions that will enable you to purchase products or use services provided by promotional partners or other sites or commerce providers. Some of those entities may share with us certain information that you provide to them.
  • We may receive data from third parties and add it to information we have collected about you. Thalamus is not responsible and assumes no liability if another site or commerce provider uses any information about you in violation of its own privacy policy, or applicable law.

We may also use personal information and de-identified data we receive from partners such as AAMC for data analysis and research that may be conducted internally for research and development purposes, including development or improvements to products, Services or Properties.
