Privacy Policy

This privacy policy (“policy”) will help you understand how Quil (Formerly SalesQ) (“us”, “we”,  “our”) uses and protects the data you provide to us when you visit and use salesq.app, salesq.ai, zoom.salesq.app, quil.ai, quil.app and zoom.quil.app (“website”, “service”). We reserve the right to change this policy at any given time. If you want to make sure that you are up to date with the latest changes, we advise you to frequently visit this page. 

Types of personal information collected

  • Your IP address and hardware identifiers
  • Your name, email address and voice biometrics (opt-in)
  • Data profile regarding your online behavior on our website.

Purpose for collecting personal information 

Why do we require “Your IP address and hardware identifiers”

  • We collect IP address information to improve session security, and flag suspicious activity.
  • We collect hardware identifiers for analytics. It’s important to know the type of device, or browser that customers are using.

Why do we require “Your name, email address and voice biometrics (opt-in)”

  • We collect names and email addresses as required identifiers for an account. This data is required for correct functioning of core features.
  • Voice biometrics refers to a user’s voice sample and is used to help identify their specific voice while on calls. It is optional and not required for the core feature set.

Why do we require “Data profile regarding your online behavior on our website.”

  • We collect behavior information while on our website. It is used for metrics and analytics to provide a better future experience for our customers.

We may send account owners marketing and product updates to their email. If you wish to opt out of this feature please reach out to support@quil.ai 

Choice and consent

We provide options for customers with varying levels of privacy concerns. We may work with trusted third parties to support features such as AI note taking. In the case of third party sub processors we will require opt-out consent to disable certain features.

Features leveraging sub processors will be disclosed and enabled by default. Changes to sub processor policy may not be reflected in our privacy policy.

Unless explicitly opted out with a third party consent. We may use or share your personal information with any other parties.

For details about our third party sub processors reach out to support@quil.ai

We collect your data for several reasons.

  • To better understand your needs
  • To improve our service and products
  • To enable better features that are useful to you.

Data Retention

Personal Information

We retain the data you submit. Meeting information including by not limited to transcriptions, notes and corrections are retained in the We system and may be retrieved at a later time.

When a service account is deleted all relevant information i’ll be removed after a maximum of 30 days. Any queries about personal information or related data can be sent to support@quil.ai with the subject line being “Questions about my personal information”

Logs

We will not log sensitive information including passwords, emails and transcriptions. 

We will log information related to system functioning, including but not limited to system metrics, timestamps, IP addresses, and requested routes. System logging is required in order to provide a reliable and preformant experience for users.

Access Control

All user and system data is considered sensitive and is not publicly accessible. Any data that the user generates is only available to that user as well as any additional users that have been allowed by the account owner. Accounts with multiple team members may share direct or derived information such as call metrics, transcriptions, models and correction information.

Disclosure and Third Parties

We may leverage trusted third party sub processors or specific features. We may require sending feature specific information to the third party. It is the responsibility of the user to read and consent to the third party’s privacy policy. We, on best effort, will keep third party policy links up to date, but ultimately it is the responsibility of the user to read up on policy changes.

If a third party sub processor discloses a security issue or data breach, We, on best efforts will notify potentially affected parties. 

Non core features that leverage sub processors may have the ability to opt-out.

We rigorously vet each sub processor for privacy and security.

Security for Privacy

We are committed to securing your data and keeping it confidential. We do all in our power to prevent data theft, unauthorized access, and disclosure by implementing the latest technologies and software, which help us safeguard all the  information we collect online. 

Personal Information is encrypted in transit, and at rest. Personal Information may also undergo additional layers of encryption depending on the sensitive nature of said information.

Google Limited Use Disclosure

Our application’s use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs will adhere to Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

We will optionally request authorized user data from Google services. The scopes of these requests are limited to reading calendar events, and requesting the authorized user email address. 

We use this additional information to facilitate automatic call scheduling and enable bots to join automatically. This feature is optional, we will never require you to grant permissions to use Quil. Calendar permissions are typically listed as “Restricted” by Google, ensuring you are allowed to authorize this application with your organization. 

Data obtained from allowing the Google integration will be shared with a trusted third party (Recall AI) for the sole purpose of scheduling future calls and allowing call bots to join automatically.

Data obtained from Google services may also be shared with relevant, trusted third parties (As mentioned in section “third party policies”) for the purpose of transcription, recording, note summarization / categorization or other product features. Examples of data usage may be speaker (participant, host) labeling, event details including descriptions, titles, or other openly available event data.
