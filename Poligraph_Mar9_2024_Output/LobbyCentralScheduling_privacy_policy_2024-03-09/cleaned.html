Technology Used

If you use your own device to access the Services, LobbyCentral may use "cookies" to enhance User experience. A cookie is a small file placed on the hard drive of your computer. You may refuse to accept browser cookies by activating the appropriate setting on your browser. However, if you select this setting you may be unable to access certain parts of the Services. Unless you have adjusted your browser setting so that it will refuse cookies, the Services will issue cookies when you direct your browser to the Services. Users may choose to set their web browser to refuse cookies or to alert you when cookies are being sent. If you do so, note that some parts of the Services may not function properly.

Additionally, certain features of the Services may use local stored objects (or Flash cookies) to collect and store information about your preferences and navigation to, from, and on the Services. The Services may also use small electronic files known as web beacons (also referred to as clear gifs, pixel tags, and single-pixel gifs) that permit LobbyCentral, for example, to count users who have visited those pages and for other related website statistics (for example, recording the popularity of certain website content and verifying system and server integrity). LobbyCentral does not currently respond to Do Not Track signals from web browsers.
