Definitions

“Data Subjects” refers to individuals whose personal data is processed or controlled by an entity or organization. These individuals have rights, as granted by applicable laws and regulations, including the right to access, correct, restrict, or erase their personal data. Upon receiving a valid request from a data subject, the responsible entity or organization takes appropriate steps to comply with the request in a timely manner, while considering any legal, regulatory, or contractual obligations.

Data Privacy Framework

Blue Sky eLearn, LLC complies with the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework (EU-U.S. DPF), the UK Extension to the EU-U.S. DPF, and the Swiss-U.S. Data Privacy Framework (Swiss-U.S. DPF) as set forth by the U.S. Department of Commerce.  Blue Sky eLearn, LLC has certified to the U.S. Department of Commerce that it adheres to the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework Principles (EU-U.S. DPF Principles) with regard to the processing of personal data received from the European Union in reliance on the EU-U.S. DPF and from the United Kingdom (and Gibraltar) in reliance on the UK Extension to the EU-U.S. DPF.  Blue Sky eLearn, LLC has certified to the U.S. Department of Commerce that it adheres to the Swiss-U.S. Data Privacy Framework Principles (Swiss-U.S. DPF Principles) with regard to the processing of personal data received from Switzerland in reliance on the Swiss-U.S. DPF.  If there is any conflict between the terms in this privacy policy and the EU-U.S. DPF Principles and/or the Swiss-U.S. DPF Principles, the Principles shall govern.  To learn more about the Data Privacy Framework (DPF) program, and to view our certification, please click here.

Legal Basis for Processing

Blue Sky eLearn processes personal data in compliance with applicable data protection laws and regulations, as outlined in the Data Protection Agreement (DPA) between the data controller and the data processor. The legal basis for processing personal data, as specified in the DPA and aligned with the privacy policy, are as follows:

  • Consent: In certain situations, we rely on the explicit consent of the individual as the legal basis for processing their personal data. For example, when an individual voluntarily provides their personal information to receive marketing communications or participate in a survey, we will obtain their consent before processing their data. Consent may be withdrawn at any time, subject to applicable legal restrictions, as outlined in the DPA.
  • Contractual Necessity: We process personal data when it is necessary to fulfill our contractual obligations with our customers or end users, as outlined in the DPA. This includes providing eLearning services, maintaining user accounts, delivering customer support, and fulfilling other services as agreed upon in our contractual agreements.
  • Legitimate Interests: We process personal data based on our legitimate business interests, ensuring that these interests do not infringe upon the rights and freedoms of the individual, as set forth in the DPA. Our legitimate interests include improving and securing our services, maintaining system integrity, conducting market research, and enhancing the user experience. We carefully balance our legitimate interests with the protection of individual privacy, ensuring compliance with applicable data protection laws and respecting user rights and expectations.
  • Legal Compliance: We process personal data when required by applicable laws and regulations, or in response to lawful requests from public authorities, as stated in the DPA. This may include fulfilling our data protection obligations, responding to subpoenas or court orders, cooperating with law enforcement agencies, or exercising and defending our legal rights.

We diligently consider and document our legal basis for processing personal data, ensuring that our processing activities align with applicable data protection laws, regulations, and the provisions specified in the DPA. We prioritize the protection of individual rights and freedoms while fulfilling our obligations and pursuing our legitimate business interests.

Data Retention

Blue Sky eLearn is committed to retaining personal data only for as long as it is necessary to fulfill the purposes for which the data was collected, in accordance with applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations. Our data retention practices adhere to the following principles:

  • Purpose Limitation: We retain personal data only for the specific purposes for which it was collected or subsequently authorized by the individual. We do not keep personal data for an indefinite period or use it for purposes that are incompatible with the original purposes of collection.
  • Retention Periods: We establish and adhere to retention periods for different categories of personal data based on the nature of the data, the purposes for which it is used, and our legal and regulatory obligations. We regularly review these retention periods to ensure they remain appropriate and up-to-date.
  • Deletion and Anonymization: When personal data is no longer needed for its original purpose or any authorized secondary purpose, we take appropriate measures to securely delete or anonymize the data. Deleted data is removed from our systems in a manner that prevents unauthorized access or accidental recovery. Anonymized data is irreversibly stripped of all personally identifiable information and may be retained for statistical or research purposes.
  • Archiving and Backups: We maintain secure backups of personal data to prevent data loss due to system failures, human error, or security breaches. Please note that we do not have the capability to modify or remove individual data entries from our backups. As such, backups retain the data as is from the time of the backup until they are eventually removed in accordance with our retention policies. The removal of backups is subject to our established data retention and deletion procedures.
  • Data Subject Rights: We respect the rights of Data Subjects to access, correct, restrict, or erase their personal data as required by applicable laws and regulations. Upon receiving a valid request from a Data Subject, we take appropriate steps to comply with the request in a timely manner, subject to any applicable legal, regulatory, or contractual obligations.

By implementing these data retention principles, Blue Sky eLearn ensures that personal data is managed responsibly, securely, and in line with the privacy expectations of our users and the requirements of applicable data protection laws.

Blue Sky eLearn adheres to the DPF Principles for all personal data received from the EU, United Kingdom, and Switzerland in reliance on the DPF.

Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach, Blue Sky eLearn, as the data controller, will promptly assess the nature and scope of the breach in accordance with the Data Protection Agreement (DPA). We will work in conjunction with the data processor to take immediate and necessary steps to contain and mitigate the impact of the breach, as specified in the DPA.

If the breach poses a significant risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, we will fulfill our obligations under the DPA and promptly notify the affected individuals and relevant supervisory authorities, as required by applicable laws and regulations. The notification will include, to the extent known or reasonably ascertainable, the relevant details of the breach, its potential consequences, and the measures taken or proposed to betaken to address the breach.

Blue Sky eLearn will collaborate closely with the data processor, as outlined in the DPA, to provide any necessary cooperation and assistance to the affected individuals and supervisory authorities, enabling them to comply with their respective obligations under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or other applicable data protection laws.

Our breach notification process, as set forth in the DPA, ensures transparency, accountability, and compliance with data protection requirements. We maintain appropriate records of all personal data breaches, including the facts surrounding the breach, its effects, and the remedial actions taken, as required by the DPA and applicable regulations.

Please note that the breach notification provisions specified in the DPA take precedence in the event of any inconsistency or conflict between this privacy policy and the DPA.

Rights of California Residents

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended, California residents are granted specific rights regarding their personal information. Blue Sky eLearn is committed to upholding these rights and ensuring transparency in our data handling practices. This Rights of California Residents provision outlines the rights granted to California residents and the steps required to exercise those rights.

  • Right to Know: California residents have the right to request information about the categories of personal information we collect, the purposes for which we use it, and the categories of third parties with whom we share it. To submit a request, residents may email us at privacy@blueskyelearn.com or complete the Data Subject Request Form available on our website.
  • Right to Access: California residents have the right to request access to the specific pieces of personal information we have collected about them. To submit an access request, residents may email us at privacy@blueskyelearn.com or complete the Data Subject Request Form available on our website.
  • Right to Delete: California residents have the right to request the deletion of their personal information from third party vendors, service providers, or contractors, subject to certain exceptions as prescribed by law. To submit a deletion request, residents may email us at privacy@blueskyelearn.com or complete the Data Subject Request Form available on our website.
  • Right to Opt-Out: California residents have the right to opt-out of the sale and sharing of their personal information with third parties, if applicable. Blue Sky eLearn does not sell personal information; therefore, no action is necessary to exercise this right.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: Blue Sky eLearn will not discriminate against California residents for exercising any of their CCPA rights, including but not limited to, denying goods or services, charging different prices, or providing a different level of quality of services.
  • Right to Restrict Use of Sensitive Personal Information:  California residents may request to limit the use and disclosure of their sensitive personal information for specific secondary purposes, including disclosure to third parties.
  • Right to Correction: California residents may request the correction of their personal information held by Blue Sky eLearn.
  • Right to Access Information about Automated Decision Making: California residents have the right to request information about automated decision-making processes based on their personal information. Data subjects may also request a description of the likely outcomes that will result from these processes.
  • Authorized Agent: California residents may designate an authorized agent to make any of the requests mentioned above on their behalf. To do so, residents must provide the authorized agent with written permission and verify their identity with us. Additionally, the authorized agent must provide proof of their authorization to act on the resident's behalf.
  • Response Time: Upon receipt of a verifiable consumer request, Blue Sky eLearn will respond to the request within 45 days. If additional time is required to process the request, Blue Sky eLearn will inform the resident of the reason for the extension and the expected response time, which will not exceed an additional 45 days.

Cookie Policy

This Privacy Policy provides information about our use of cookies across our websites including our main webpage (www.blueskyelearn.com) and our main product's webpage (www.pathlms.com/showroom). Cookies are small files that are sent to your web browser when you visit a website. They serve a range of purposes, including authenticating users, enabling efficient navigation, storing user preferences, and enhancing the overall user experience on our site.  Cookies on the Blue Sky eLearn websites do lots of different jobs, like authenticate you when visiting the websites, letting you navigate between pages efficiently, storing your preferences, storing your settings and preferences of video playback functionality, and generally improving your experience on the site.

The Blue Sky eLearn websites can also use some non-essential cookies. We do not do this to track individual users or to identify them, but to gain useful knowledge about how the sites and their content are used so that we can keep improving them for our users. Without the knowledge we gain from the systems that use these cookies, we would not be able to provide the service we do.

Blue Sky likes to understand how visitors use our websites by using web analytics services. They count the number of visitors and tell us things about the visitors’ behavior overall – such as the typical length of stay on the site, the average number of pages a user views, what media content a user consumes on the site, and similar behavior and viewing patterns. Information collected through these cookies are not used to identify users, thus no personal information is collected and data is used as aggregated information only so that an individual user may not be identified.

  • Essential cookies: These cookies are crucial for you to navigate our websites and use our features, such as accessing secure areas and providing services you have requested. Opting out of these cookies is not available if you want to continue using our services.

The collected data is used to enhance our websites' performance. To opt out of these cookies, please follow the instructions provided under "How to manage cookies."

Once you log into your account or provide your contact information, the data gathered by these cookies is linked to your provided contact information. To opt out of these cookies, follow the instructions under "Managing Cookies."
