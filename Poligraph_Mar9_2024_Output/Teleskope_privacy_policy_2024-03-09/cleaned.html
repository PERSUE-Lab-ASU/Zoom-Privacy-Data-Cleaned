Data Collection

Teleskope may collect and receive Data in a variety of ways:

  1. Personal Data from Authorized Users. Customers or individuals granted access to an ERG account by a Customer (“Authorized Users”) routinely submit the Data to Teleskope when using the Services. The following Data is collected and stored for purposes of providing Services:

    • Name

    • Profile Picture

    • Email

    • Office Location

    • Department

    • ERG Membership

  2. Personal Data as part of Customer Account Information. To use Teleskope Services, Customers and individuals provide Teleskope with various information necessary to create a company account such as company domain name, company logo, contact email address, contact name, contact title, contact office location, password and/or similar account details. In addition, Customers that purchase a paid version of the Services provide Teleskope (or its payment processors) with billing details such as credit card information, banking information and/or a billing address. Though in most cases that information is business or proprietary information of Customers, rather than personal data, sometimes some personal data may constitute some part of that information.

  3. Usage Information as follows:

    • Services Metadata. When an Authorized User interacts with the Services, metadata is generated that provides additional context about the way Authorized Users work and for troubleshooting and reporting purposes. For example, Teleskope logs the Website links you interact with, emails sent to you, Services methods invoked.

    • Log data. As with most websites and technology services delivered over the Internet, our servers automatically collect information when you access or use our Websites or Services and record it in log files. This log data may include the Internet Protocol (IP) address, the address of the web page visited before using the Website or Services, browser type and settings, the date and time the Services were used, information about browser configuration and plugins, language preferences and cookie data.

    • Device information. Teleskope collects information about devices accessing the Services, including type of device, what operating system is used, device settings, application IDs, unique device identifiers and crash data. Whether we collect some or all of this Other Information often depends on the type of device used and its settings.

    • Location information. We receive information from you, your Customer and other third-parties that helps us approximate your location. For this purpose, we may, for example, use a business address submitted by your employer, time zone provided by your device or browser, or an IP address received from your browser or device to determine approximate location. Teleskope may also collect location information from devices in accordance with the consent process provided by your device. We never use GPS of similar location tracking technologies.

  4. Cookie Information. Teleskope uses cookies and similar technologies on our Websites and Services. The Websites and Services may also include cookies and similar tracking technologies of third parties, which may collect some Data via the Websites and Services and across other websites and online services. We use the cookies to maintain your session so that we do not have to prompt you for login un-necessarily, to save your preferences and to store data between requests.

  5. Third Party Services. Customer can choose to permit or restrict its Authorized Users to enable and disable Third Party Services for their ERGs and other Services. Typically, Third Party Services are software and platforms that integrate with our Services which may share with us some personal data of you or other persons. For example, if a Yammer application is enabled to permit messages to be imported to an ERG account, we may receive user name and email address of Authorized Users, along with additional information that the application has elected to make available to Teleskope to facilitate the integration. Authorized Users should check the privacy settings and notices of those Third Party Services to understand what data may be disclosed to Teleskope. When a Third Party Service is enabled, Teleskope is authorized to connect and access any personal data made available to Teleskope in accordance with our agreement with the Third Party Provider. We do not, however, receive or store passwords for any of these Third Party Services when connecting them to the Services.

  6. Location & Media. Subject to your consent as express in the settings of your device, we may get access location or pictures or videos that an Authorized User chooses to provide (such as event picture uploaded from a device, or location information provided to search events nearby).

  7. Data from Third Sources. Teleskope may receive information about organizations, industries, events, and other matters related to our business from affiliates, partners or others that we use to make our Services better or more useful. This information may be in some way used together with other information which may accidentally contain the Data we process. Such information might include general data, such as which IP addresses correspond to zip codes or countries. Or it might be more specific: For example, when creating an event in an ERG account, we will search for nearby locations that match the search string and that are close to the location of the organizer, to allow the organizer to choose a venue for the event.

  8. Additional Information Provided to Teleskope. We may receive Data when submitted to our Websites or if you participate in a focus group, contest, activity or event, apply for a job, request support, interact with our social media accounts or otherwise communicate with Teleskope.

  9. Sensitive Data. We do not seek to collect or otherwise process sensitive data as set out in Article 9 of the GDPR. Furthermore, we encourage you to be particularly careful with sharing such information via the Services, either publicly or via direct messages, or as your account information, since erasure or removal such information from public access or otherwise may not be necessarily guaranteed by us in light of our legitimate interests, such as preserving integrity of communications, and burdens of such erasure or removal.
